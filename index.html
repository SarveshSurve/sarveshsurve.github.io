A short intro

The scientist behind the work.

I'm a PhD candidate at the Geisel School of Medicine at Dartmouth, working in Dr. George O'Toole's lab. My research sits at the intersection of microbial ecology, gut microbiome science, and translational medicine — focused on how gut bacteria can improve outcomes in cystic fibrosis (CF).

My thesis centers on Bacteroides as a potential CF probiotic. I combine experimental evolution, molecular microbiology, culturomics, and bioinformatics to understand how these bacteria adapt in the CF gut.

Before Dartmouth, I trained in India at Symbiosis International University and the University of Mumbai, developing expertise in lactic acid bacteria, probiotic science, and secondary metabolite biosynthesis. In 2025, I attended the Marine Biological Laboratory's Microbial Diversity course at Woods Hole.

Outside the lab, I'm chasing National Parks, Formula 1 races, and the perfect data visualization.
